Every Iteration of Brimstone

A Character Design Map

The first recorded instance of Brimstone


Brimstone began to take shape when the junior class of the illustration department started the Mask Project.
We were sent to the Nelson Atkins Museum of Art to look for inspiration. I ended up in the Native American exhibit,
and this mask in particular struck me. I enjoyed drawing it so much that it became the root of inspiration for my
mask project.

Brimstone: Born


The design stage of the Mask Project began. I needed to design a mask that expressed "schadenfreude." So I
began to sketch. This is where the mask design started to turn into a character. Because once the mask was designed,
I wanted to design the outfit to wear with it, too. And once I drew the outfit, I wanted to doodle the character
and build a world around her. By the end of the project, Brimstone was a full-fledged character and the worldbuilding
was just beginning...

Brimstone: Exploration


Once Brimstone became a concrete concept, I drew her all the time. It was during this period of consistently
sketching her that the story started to naturally develop. Most of it was vague (post-apocalyptic, religious themes,
plot points revolving around dealing with grief), but the best part of this era was the concept art. I drew Brimstone
in many different mediums, trying out both loose and detailed expressions of her character.

Brimstone: The Game


Eventually, I wanted to develop Brimstone into something more than just drawings. At the time, I had recently played
a cult classic game called OFF. The game was made by a single person in an old, pixel graphics RPG engine,
no coding required. It single-handedly inspired me to turn Brimstone into a video game, even though I had never done
anything like it before. Over the next two years, I worked a lot in RPG Maker 2003 (the exact engine that was
used to make OFF) and Brimstone went through several artistic iterations in pixel form.

That's all, folks!
